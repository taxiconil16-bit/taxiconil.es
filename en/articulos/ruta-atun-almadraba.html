1. What is Almadraba? The Art of Patience

Almadraba is a millennia-old, selective, and sustainable fishing technique practiced for over 3,000 years. It consists of a labyrinth of nets that intercept tuna during their migration from the Atlantic to the Mediterranean. It is a method that respects the animal's life cycle and guarantees supreme meat quality, capturing only adult specimens returning to spawn.

Almadraba: a ingenious net system that survives the passage of centuries with respect for the sea.

Did you know... The term "ronqueo" comes from the almost metallic sound made by the knife when rubbing against the tuna's backbone during manual butchering. It is a visual and auditory spectacle that leaves no one indifferent and requires years of practice.
