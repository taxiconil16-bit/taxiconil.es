1. Setenil de las Bodegas: The town under the rock

Setenil is no ordinary town. Here, architecture does not compete with nature but surrenders to it. Its houses are not built on the mountain, but inside it, taking advantage of the gorges excavated by the Guadalporcún river over millennia.

Calle Cuevas del Sol: where the rock becomes the natural roof for the residents.

Strolling through Calle Cuevas del Sol and Calle Cuevas de la Sombra is a surreal experience. Tons of solid rock loom over your head while you enjoy a tapa of local produce. It is, without doubt, one of the most singular urban landscapes in the world.

Did you know... The name Setenil comes from the Latin "Septem nihil" (seven times nothing), in honour of the seven sieges the Christians had to perform to reconquer the villa.

2. Ronda: The dream city over the Tajo

Just 20 minutes from Setenil lies Ronda, one of the oldest and most romantic cities in Spain. Divided by a chasm nearly 100 metres deep, the city seems suspended in the air, offering an unparalleled visual spectacle.

The New Bridge of Ronda: a titanic work that joins history with the abyss.

The Puente Nuevo (New Bridge) is the undisputed symbol of the city. Connecting the old town with the new required an 18th-century engineering work that today is the most photographed spot in Andalusia. Furthermore, its Bullring and Nasrid palaces hold legends of bandits and poets.
